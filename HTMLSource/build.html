Integrated Learning Centre (ILC)

Home to the Engineering Society

Thursday 7:30AM - 11:00PM
Friday 7:30AM - 5:30PM
Saturday 11:30AM - 7:00PM
Sunday 11:30AM - 11:00PM

Features

Computer Lab

There are computer labs that seat multiple students for laboratories that require computer use. These labs also possess electronic test equipment for other laboratory activities dealing with electricity and circuits.

Design Studio

Teaching and design studios are used for a smaller class teaching style that are used for classes involving computer use and CAD design.

Active Learning Room

The active learning room is a flexible teaching space with movable tables and chairs. There are often extra help sessions held in this room weekly for students to get assistance from upper year students.

The ILC contains 38 individual group rooms that are available to all engineering students to reserve to work on group activities whenever needed. Students are given a set amount of hours per semester to book rooms.

Clark Hall

Engineering Hub

Clark Hall is home to multiple different services, which are explained below:

Clark Hall Pub

Clark Hall Pub, also known as the "centre of the universe" is the oldest pub on campus and was established as a place for students and professors to interact outside of the classroom. The pub hosts free live music shows on the small stage inside and its most well-known event, "Ritual." Ritual is a weekly event held every Friday at the pub where many engineering students from all current years will gather together at Clark and enjoy some beverages and unwind from the previous week. Most engineers will join in on ritual at least once (drinking is not required to attend) and alumni will also sometimes join in on ritual when they are in town.
Campus Equipment Outfitters (CEO)

Located on the second floor of Clark Hall is the main office of CEO, which is the location on campus to buy engineering apparel. This is also where engineers will order the famous Queen's Applied Science jacket, or GPA (Golden Party Armour).

Campus Bookstore

The Campus Bookstore is operated by students under the Queen's University Engineering Society Services Incorporated as a not-for-profit organization. The bookstore sells many different items including textbooks, school supplies, study aids, Queen's apparel, etc.
Golden Words

Clark Hall also houses the office of Golden Words, which is the satire newspaper for engineers.

The Queen's Centre hosts a full food court that includes the following establishments: - Tim Hortons - Teriyaki Experience - Pizza Pizza - Booster Juice

This small grocery store carries all the essentials of a grocery store to allow students to purchase food and ingrediants without having to leave campus to visit an actual grocery store.

Pharmacy

This small pharmacy carries all the normal amenities of a pharmacy, including various goods that students may need to purchase, standard medications and a pharmacist so students do not need to leave campus to pick up prescriptions when required.

Athletics and Recreation Centre (ARC)

The ARC is a state of the art fitness facility which features a wide array of programs and activities for the campus and Kingston community. The main feature of the ARC is its extensive fitness zone. It contains over 24,500 square feet of area dedicated to fitness, which includes the latest fitness equipment and over 10 tons of weights. The ARC also offers many other fitness options besides straight fitness equipment. It houses Kingston's largest pool, measuring in at 38m x 25m. There are two full gymnasiums in the facility, equipped with spectator stands that are available for casual recreation or rental. Additionally there are four full dance studios, eight squash courts, two racquetball courts and two combative rooms for martial arts and wrestling. Finally the facility is also equipped with large locker rooms and team rooms. The ARC has very reasonable monthly rates, or is free to students who have paid the necessary student fees in their tuition.

Queen's Innovation Complex (QIC)

Engineering Innovation


**The above image is only a design concept**

The Queen's Innovation Complex (QIC) is a building that aims to become the campus hub for the expertise and collaborative environment that engineers need to become innovators and entrepreneurs. The building is planned to cost $45 million dollars, will be approximately 70,000 square feet and will have a futuristic vibe to it. The new high tech teaching environment and design studios will be modelled after the very successful spaces in the Integrated Learning Centre. The building will house specialized classrooms, offices and equipment. The complex will also house two floors of laboratory space, which will support over 20 faculty researchers and the graduate students working under them in their research. Overall the QIC aims to provide space where innovation and creativity will thrive and grow as such spaces are currently very limited and spread around campus. Finally the building will expand opportunities for students by mixing engineers with students from all faculties and disciplines. There will also be a mixing of undergraduate and graduate students to promote innovation and synergy of ideas.

"Whether or not they plan to start a business, and regardless of their field of study, today's graduates need to be entrepreneurial," says Principal Daniel Woolf. "They need to think critically and creatively and be able to analyze and assess complex problems. That's what entrepreneurs do. And that's what every student must do to succeed in today's competitive world."

The QIC also is also going to ensure that Queen's is a valuable contributor to the Canadian economy. Additionally it will propel Canada's global standing as an innovative nation. The Innovation Complex plans to make Queen's even stronger as an institution that helps to advance Canada's political, social and economic well-being. Lastly, QIC will be able to enhance the roles of graduates in the global marketplace, which is very beneficial.
